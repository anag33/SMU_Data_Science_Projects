Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

There is a positive correlation between Poverty and Health Risks. The scatterplot shows that as poverty and the lack of healthcare percentage increases so does the healthcare risks. There is a strong relationship between poverty and obesity.

There is a moderate negative correlation between Age and Health Risks. A moderate negative cluster pattern can be observed between age and lack of healthcare with a few outliers. Texas stands out with 24 percent of the median age demographics lacking healthcare.

There is a weak negative correlation between median Household Income and Health Risks. Obesity and Smokes both show a stronger correlation with Household Income.
